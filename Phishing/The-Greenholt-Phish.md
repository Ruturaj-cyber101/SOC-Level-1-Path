# The Greenholt Phish

## Overview
This room provided a practical phishing investigation scenario simulating a real-world SOC case. The objective was to analyze a suspicious email campaign, identify malicious artifacts, and determine the overall threat impact.

The lab emphasized applying structured investigation techniques in a simulated enterprise environment.

---

## Scenario Summary

A suspicious phishing email was reported within an organization. The task involved analyzing the message, identifying Indicators of Compromise (IOCs), and determining whether the email posed a legitimate security threat.

The investigation required reviewing technical evidence and applying analytical reasoning from a SOC analyst perspective.

---

## Key Investigation Areas

### 1. Email Analysis
- Reviewing sender information
- Identifying impersonation attempts
- Analyzing suspicious language and urgency tactics
- Detecting social engineering elements

### 2. Artifact Extraction
- Extracting embedded URLs
- Identifying suspicious domains
- Reviewing attachment details (if present)

### 3. IOC Identification
- Malicious domains
- Suspicious IP addresses
- Spoofed sender addresses
- Potential credential harvesting infrastructure

### 4. Validation & Enrichment
- Checking domain reputation
- Verifying infrastructure details
- Correlating evidence with threat intelligence sources

---

## Investigation Workflow Applied

1. Review the reported phishing email  
2. Inspect sender and domain details  
3. Extract URLs and analyze destination behavior  
4. Validate artifacts using reputation tools  
5. Identify confirmed IOCs  
6. Assess impact and determine severity  
7. Document findings for escalation

Case-based investigations simulate real SOC workflows. The ability to analyze phishing campaigns from start to finish improves:
